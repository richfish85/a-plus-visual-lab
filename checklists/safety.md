# Safety Checklist

## What

This checklist keeps hardware, network, and security practice safe.

## Why

Hands-on work is useful, but some actions can damage equipment or expose private data.

Example:

Opening a desktop case is fine when it is powered off and unplugged. Opening a power supply is not part of this lab.

## How

Hardware safety:

- [ ] Power off before opening a computer.
- [ ] Unplug power before touching internal parts.
- [ ] Use an ESD strap or touch grounded metal first.
- [ ] Do not open power supplies.
- [ ] Keep liquids away from the bench.

Network safety:

- [ ] Use a lab network or simulator.
- [ ] Do not scan networks without permission.
- [ ] Record fake IP addresses when publishing notes.
- [ ] Do not change shared router settings without permission.

Security safety:

- [ ] Do not download real malware.
- [ ] Use sample screenshots for phishing practice.
- [ ] Use dummy accounts.
- [ ] Do not publish secrets, keys, or passwords.

Checklist:

- [ ] Safe power state.
- [ ] Safe network target.
- [ ] Safe data.
- [ ] Safe evidence.
