1 / What happened

A ticket was closed, but the notes do not explain the problem, action, or result.

Symptoms
Status closed / blank resolution / confused user
Applies to
Service desk ticket closure
First rule
A ticket is not done until the next person can follow it
2 / Why it happens

Most cases are missing resolution, early closure, or notes that are too vague for the user or next technician.

3 / Diagnose

Is the resolution missing?

  1. Read the resolution field.Check for the actual fix, not just "done" or "resolved".
  2. Check the timeline.Look for symptoms, checks, actions, and user replies.
  3. Check user confirmation.Was the result confirmed or only assumed?
  4. List missing facts.Problem, checks, action, result, owner, next step.
3 / Diagnose

Was the ticket closed too early?

  1. Check current user status.Ask whether the issue is resolved now.
  2. Check reopen history.Repeated reopen means closure failed.
  3. Check linked tickets.Work may have moved to another ticket.
  4. Confirm ownership.Decide whether to reopen, reassign, or escalate.
3 / Diagnose

Are notes too unclear?

  1. Read as a new technician.Can you tell what was tested and changed?
  2. Read as the user.Can they understand the outcome?
  3. Find vague words.Replace "fixed", "checked", and acronyms with exact actions.
  4. Check for next step.Every unresolved ticket needs an owner and next action.
4 / Fix safely

Add factual resolution

Do not invent checks that did not happen.
  1. Review ticket history.
  2. Add known facts only.
  3. Contact user or technician if facts are missing.
  4. Add resolution note and user-facing summary.
  5. Verify: ticket explains problem, checks, action, and result.
4 / Fix safely

Reopen or redirect

Do not close again without evidence.
  1. Reopen if policy allows.
  2. Move ticket to Diagnose, Waiting, or Escalated.
  3. Add note explaining why it was reopened.
  4. Contact user with current owner and next step.
  5. Verify: owner and next action are clear.
4 / Fix safely

Rewrite in plain language

Keep technical detail, but make it readable.
  1. Keep exact checks and results.
  2. Replace jargon with plain terms.
  3. Add user-facing outcome.
  4. Add escalation or reopen path.
  5. Verify: a new tech can continue work.
5 / Document it

Record what was missing, what was corrected, and whether the ticket is resolved or still owned.
