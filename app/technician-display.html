Back to runbook
1 / What happened

The desktop has power, but the monitor shows no image.

Symptoms
Fans spin / power light on / screen blank
Applies to
Desktop PC with separate monitor
First rule
Check monitor, input, and cables before opening the PC
2 / Why it happens

Most cases are monitor power, wrong input, loose cable, or a hardware fault. Start outside the case.

3 / Diagnose

Is the monitor getting power?

  1. Check monitor power light.On: move to signal checks. Off: keep checking power.
  2. Press monitor power once.Do not hold buttons or change menu settings yet.
  3. Follow the power cable.Check the monitor end, wall socket, and power board.
  4. Test the wall socket.Use a known-working low-risk device such as a phone charger.
3 / Diagnose

Is the signal reaching the monitor?

  1. Check monitor input.Match the selected input to the cable: HDMI, DisplayPort, or VGA.
  2. Reseat both video cable ends.Check the monitor end and desktop end.
  3. Check the correct PC port.If the PC has a graphics card, use the graphics card port, not the motherboard port.
  4. Swap to a known-good cable.If the new cable works, label or remove the failed cable.
3 / Diagnose

Is the monitor or PC hardware at fault?

  1. Test the monitor on a known-good device.If it stays blank, the monitor is likely faulty.
  2. Listen for POST beeps.Record beep or LED patterns exactly.
  3. Try another video output if allowed.If one port works and another does not, record exactly which port failed.
  4. Stop before risky internal work.Escalate if not trained or not authorised.
4 / Fix safely

Restore monitor power

Do not open a monitor or power board. Replace, do not repair.
  1. Reseat the power cable at the monitor and wall.
  2. Bypass the power board if safe and allowed.
  3. Move to a working wall socket if the socket is dead.
  4. Swap the monitor if it has no power on a known-good socket.
  5. Verify: image stays visible for one minute.
4 / Fix safely

Restore video signal

Power the PC down before moving stubborn video cables.
  1. Select the input that matches the connected cable.
  2. Reseat the video cable at both ends.
  3. Move the cable to the graphics card port if the PC has one.
  4. Replace the cable if a known-good cable fixes the display.
  5. Verify: image survives one restart.
4 / Fix safely

Escalate or reseat hardware

Unplug power and use ESD care before touching internal parts.
  1. Power off and unplug the PC.
  2. Reseat graphics card only if trained and allowed.
  3. Reconnect graphics card power cables if present.
  4. Tag and swap a failed monitor.
  5. Escalate: POST codes, RAM, board, or PSU faults.
5 / Document it

Use short ticket notes. State symptom, checks, action, result, and what was not changed.

User reported desktop has power but monitor shows no image.
Confirmed desktop power light and fans active.
Checked monitor power and selected input.
Checked video cable at monitor and desktop.
Reseated HDMI cable.
Display returned after input was changed to HDMI 1.
No internal hardware changes made.
