# Laptops And Mobile Devices

## What

Laptops and mobile devices are portable computers.

That group includes laptops, tablets, and phones that people carry, dock, sync, and use on the move.

## Why

This topic matters because A+ learners need to recognise the differences in form factor, power use, connectivity, and user expectations.

Portable devices often blur together in everyday language, but the hardware and support needs are not the same.

## How

- A laptop is a full computer in a portable form.
- A tablet is usually touch-first and lighter on physical ports.
- A phone or tablet may rely heavily on cloud sync and wireless services.
- Device setup often includes account sign-in, syncing, backup, and security settings.

Useful framing:

- Laptop = portable workstation.
- Tablet = touch-focused portable device.
- Phone = communication-first portable device.
- Sync and backup are part of normal support, not extra features.
