# Wireless Networks

## What

Wireless networks let devices connect without a cable.

In practice, most learners meet this as Wi-Fi on a home, school, or office network.

## Why

Wireless is everywhere, so A+ learners need to understand both the convenience and the extra risk it brings.

That makes wireless one of the most practical networking topics to learn early.

## How

- A wireless access point or wireless router provides radio coverage.
- A Wi-Fi client connects from within range.
- Strong passwords and encryption matter because the signal travels through the air.
- Wireless is convenient, but the device still depends on the same network ideas as wired networking.

Useful framing:

- Wired and wireless are both network access.
- Wi-Fi is the common WLAN technology.
- Good security settings matter more because nearby devices can attempt to join.
