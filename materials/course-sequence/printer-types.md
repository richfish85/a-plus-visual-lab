# Printer Types

What

Printer types usually break down by print technology and feature set. The big split is inkjet versus laser. A second split is single-function versus multifunction. For the learner, the important thing is not the brand name, but what the device is best at and what it tends to cost to run.

Why

This topic helps the learner match the right printer to the job. It also makes troubleshooting easier, because inkjet and laser devices fail in different ways and use different consumables.

How

Use these comparisons:

- Inkjet: good for photos, mixed color work, and lower-volume home or small office use.
- Laser: good for fast text printing, higher-volume document work, and lower per-page cost in many office setups.
- Single-function: prints only.
- Multifunction: prints plus scan, copy, and sometimes fax.

Useful support clues:

- Inkjet problems often involve clogged nozzles, dried ink, or cartridge issues.
- Laser problems often involve toner, drums, fusers, or paper feed problems.
- MFDs add extra paths for scanning and copying, so the issue may not be printing at all.
