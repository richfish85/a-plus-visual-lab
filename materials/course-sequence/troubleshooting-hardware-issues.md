# Troubleshooting Hardware Issues

What

Hardware troubleshooting means checking physical parts and their connections before assuming the whole device has failed. Common examples are power, RAM, storage, display, ports, fans, keyboard, mouse, and printer hardware.

Why

Learners need a practical way to sort out "does not power on" from "powers on but misbehaves". The answer changes depending on whether the issue is cable, component, driver, or a deeper failure.

How

Use a simple hardware checklist:

- Confirm power source and cable.
- Watch for lights, fans, beeps, or display output.
- Reseat obvious components when safe to do so.
- Try a known-good cable, display, or peripheral.
- Check Device Manager and built-in diagnostics if the system starts.
- Replace the smallest likely faulty part first.

Common examples:

- No power: outlet, PSU, battery, charger, or power button.
- No video: cable, monitor, port, or graphics adapter.
- Random shutdowns: heat, fan, or power instability.
- Missing device: bad cable, disabled port, driver, or failed hardware.
