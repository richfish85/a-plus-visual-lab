# Printers And MFDs

What

Printers turn digital pages into paper. An MFD, or multifunction device, combines printing with one or more of scan, copy, and fax. In support work, the first job is usually to identify the device type, how it connects, and whether the problem is paper, power, network, driver, or consumables.

Why

This topic gives the learner a practical view of office printing without turning it into a vendor brochure. It also helps connect the hardware view to Windows troubleshooting, because many printer problems are connection or driver problems rather than "printer is broken" problems.

How

Start with the basics:

- Check whether the device is inkjet or laser.
- Check whether it is single-function or multifunction.
- Check whether it is USB, Ethernet, Wi-Fi, or shared over a print server.
- Check common consumables like toner, ink, paper, and waste containers.
- Check status lights, display messages, and print queue errors.

In a simple help desk flow, we usually think:

1. Power and cable.
2. Paper and consumables.
3. Queue and driver.
4. Network or sharing.
5. Restart or reinstall if needed.
