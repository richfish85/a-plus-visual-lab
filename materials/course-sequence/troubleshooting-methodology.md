# Troubleshooting Methodology

What

Troubleshooting methodology is the repeatable way we solve a problem without guessing. In A+ terms, the goal is to identify the symptom, narrow the cause, test one change at a time, and confirm the fix.

Why

This is the core skill behind almost every support task. A steady process saves time, reduces repeat work, and stops learners from jumping straight to random fixes.

How

A simple support flow looks like this:

1. Identify the problem.
2. Gather information and duplicate the issue.
3. Decide what is most likely causing it.
4. Test one likely fix.
5. Verify the result.
6. Document what happened.

Helpful habits:

- Ask what changed.
- Check the obvious first.
- Change one thing at a time.
- Reproduce the issue after each change.
- Keep notes so the next person does not start from zero.
