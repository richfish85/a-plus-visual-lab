# Troubleshooting Printer Devices

What

Printer troubleshooting is the work of finding out why a printer will not print, prints badly, prints slowly, or appears offline. Common causes are paper jams, empty consumables, bad connections, driver issues, queue problems, or a stuck spooler.

Why

Printers can look complicated, but most failures are plain and mechanical. The learner benefits from a short checklist that starts with the physical device and then moves inward to drivers and queues.

How

Use this order:

- Check power, paper, and ink or toner.
- Clear paper jams and close covers.
- Check USB, Ethernet, Wi-Fi, or Bluetooth connection.
- Print a test page.
- Clear the print queue if jobs are stuck.
- Update or reinstall the printer driver if needed.
- Check the printer troubleshooter and manufacturer support if the issue stays.

Typical signs:

- Printer not found or not recognized.
- Stuck queue or print spooler failure.
- Offline status or shared printer not reachable.
- Blank pages, streaks, faded text, or poor alignment.
- Paper jam or tray feed errors.
