# Troubleshooting Networks

What

Network troubleshooting means finding out why a device cannot connect, cannot reach the internet, or cannot reach a specific service. The problem might be Wi-Fi, Ethernet, DNS, IP addressing, a router, a firewall, or a service outage.

Why

This topic matters because many "network" failures are really connection, address, or name-resolution problems. A learner who can sort those layers quickly can avoid chasing the wrong fix.

How

Start with the visible checks:

- Confirm the device is connected to the right network.
- Check Wi-Fi, cable, port, and airplane mode or adapter state.
- Test another site, then another device.
- Check IP address, gateway, and DNS.
- Reset the adapter or network stack if needed.
- Restart the router or access point if the issue is local.

Typical signs:

- Connected but no internet.
- No network icon or limited connectivity.
- Slow page loads or only some sites failing.
- DNS errors.
- Printer, file share, or app access failing even though Wi-Fi looks fine.
